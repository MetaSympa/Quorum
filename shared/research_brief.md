## 1. Club History and Heritage

### Founding
- **Established**: 1938
- **Official Name**: Deshapriya Park Sarbojanin Durgotsav (also referred to as Deshapriya Park Durgotsab Samity)
- **Type**: Sarbojanin (community/public) Durga Puja
- **Age**: 87 years as of 2025

### Heritage and Significance
- One of the oldest and most prestigious Durga Puja celebrations in South Kolkata.
- Part of the broader Kolkata Durga Puja tradition that received UNESCO recognition as Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity (inscribed 2021).
- The puja ground at Deshapriya Park is a major cultural landmark in the Ballygunge/Rashbehari area.

### Record-Breaking Achievement (2015)
- Unveiled the **world's tallest Durga idol at 88 feet**.
- Material: Fiberglass and cement (non-traditional).
- Sculptor: **Mintu Pal** and team.
- Budget reported at approximately Rs 250 crore (for the full celebration).
- Listed in: **Limca Book of Records**, **Indian Book of Records**, and **Asia Book of Records**.
- The 2015 event drew such massive crowds that a stampede incident occurred, underlining the puja's enormous popularity.

## 2. Club Activities

### Annual Durga Puja Celebrations
The club is known for large-scale, theme-based pandals and idols that attract millions of visitors each year. Key aspects:
- **Theme-based pandals** with elaborate artistic installations
- **Collaboration with Chandannagar lighting artists** for dynamic LED, neon, and moving light displays
- **Cultural programs**: Musical nights, performances by reputed artists, dance, and theatre on the park grounds
- **Eco-friendly initiatives**: Use of biodegradable materials, natural colors, and energy-efficient lighting

### Themes by Year
| Year | Theme / Highlight |
|------|-------------------|
| 2025 | "Boro Durga" (Giant Durga idol revival) / "Jalchobi" |
| 2024 | "Bhubaneswari" / "Universal Shakti -- The Power that Connects All" |
| 2023 | "Jyoti" |
| 2022 | "Bhubaneswari" |
| 2017 | Mahishmati palace (inspired by the film Baahubali) |
| 2016 | Idol with 1,000 hands slaying 100 demons |
| 2015 | World's tallest Durga idol (88 feet) |

### Community and Social Work
- Blood donation camps
- Free health check-up camps for the underprivileged
- Clothing distribution drives
- Relief efforts during natural disasters
- Dengue awareness campaigns

## 3. Latest News (2024-2025)

### Durga Puja 2024
- **Theme**: "Universal Shakti -- The Power that Connects All" (also reported as "Bhubaneswari")
- **Artist/Sculptor**: Pradip Rudra Pal crafted the idols
- **Pandal design**: Blend of Indian temple artistry and contemporary abstract installations, with intricate carvings, fiber art, and eco-friendly materials
- **Lighting**: Chandannagar-style dynamic storytelling through LED patterns, neon designs, and moving light displays narrating mythological tales and social messages
- **Crowd management**: Dedicated entry/exit points with Kolkata Police collaboration; accessible via Kalighat, Jatin Das Park, and Rabindra Sarobar metro stations

### Durga Puja 2025 (Preview)
- **Theme**: "Boro Durga" -- a revival of the spectacular giant Durga idol concept that made headlines a decade ago
- **Opening date**: 28 September 2025
- **Concept**: Aims to recreate the awe and nostalgia of the 2015 record-breaking celebration with a towering, artistically intricate idol

### Awards
- The club regularly competes in major Kolkata Durga Puja award circuits including:
  - **Biswa Bangla Sharad Samman** (state government award, est. 2013)
  - **ABP Ananda Sharod Arghya**
  - **CESC The Telegraph True Spirit Puja**
- Specific award wins for individual years were not confirmed in publicly available sources at the time of research.

## Summary for Dashboard Landing Page

Deshapriya Park Sarbojanin Durgotsav is an 87-year-old institution (est. 1938) and one of South Kolkata's most iconic Durga Puja celebrations. It gained international fame in 2015 with the world's tallest Durga idol (88 feet, entered three record books). The club is known for ambitious theme-based pandals, Chandannagar-style lighting, and active community service including health camps and disaster relief. The 2024 celebration featured the "Universal Shakti" theme with sculptor Pradip Rudra Pal, and the 2025 edition revives the "Boro Durga" giant idol concept. The organizing body -- Deshapriya Park Durgotsab Samity -- operates from the Deshapriya Park grounds near Rash Behari Avenue, Kolkata 700029.
